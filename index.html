Aim
The EASE-T (Evaluation and Assessment in computer Science Education and Training) 2023 workshop aims to bring together researchers and educators from both academic and industry to discuss and advance the state of the art in Computer Science education and training, regardless of the area of specialization of present and future computer scientists. In other words, the goal of EASE-T is to assess and evaluate consolidated educational and training means to let present and future computer scientists acquire the required soft and hard skills, as well as to experiment (and then assess and evaluate) novel educational and training means, including the use of any resource or technology for educational and training purposes in the Computer Science field. EASE-T 2023 is also interested in evaluating and assessing both soft and hard skills required to computer scientists, as well as engagement and retention (e.g. gender balance) in Computer Science education and training.

Scope
The study of Computer Science leads to different job opportunities e.g. computer scientists can specialize as software developers, software engineers, database administrators, etc Besides these traditional job opportunities, there are emerging areas in Computer Science e.g. artificial intelligence and cybersecurity representing new job opportunities for computer scientists. However, Computer Science is not easy to teach and learn, and it is not accessible to everyone. Understanding how to improve aspects related to learning, teaching, and accessibility of this discipline is of primary relevance to educators and researchers, and even more to the labor maker, which demands an ever-increasing number of computer scientists with a rich set of soft and hard skills. Gathering empirical evidence by using quantitative, qualitative, and mixed research is a necessary step to increase our body of knowledge in Computer Science education and training. Given the expertise of EASE attendees in empirical research, we believe the EASE community can provide valuable feedback about the evaluation and assessment in the Computer Science education and training context.

Topics of Interest
EASE-T 2023 encourages contributions covering any topic related to (quantitative, qualitative, and mixed) research in the context of Computer Science education and training. The topics of interest include, but are not limited to:
  • Educational and training methods for acquiring Computer Science skills (e.g. programming languages, hardware and software technologies, software development, etc) and soft skills (e.g. problem-solving, teamwork, stress management etc) required to computer scientists
  • Role of soft skills and human factors in Computer Science education and training
  • Measurement of Computer Science skills, as well as soft skills required to computer scientists, through artificial intelligence methods and not only
  • Pedagogical approaches supporting Computer Science education and training in distributed and remote settings
  • Education and training of computer scientists in university and workplace settings
  • Online platforms and software tools specially designed or just used for education and training purposes in Computer Science
  • Means (e.g. coding clubs, boot camps, contests, and serious games) for introducing Computer Science to primary and secondary students
  • Certification of computer scientists
  • Continuing training in Computer Science
  • Engagement and retention (e.g. gender balance) in Computer Science education and training
  • Any education and training means for present and future computer scientists not listed above
